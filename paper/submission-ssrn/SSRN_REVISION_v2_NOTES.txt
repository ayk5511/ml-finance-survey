SSRN Revision Notes for Paper 1, Version 2
============================================

Title: Machine Learning in Quantitative Finance: A Systematic Review of
       Methods, Applications, and Open Challenges (2015-2025)

SSRN Abstract ID: 6562398
Author: Akram Khan
Original submission: 2026-04-12
Revision date: 2026-04-27
File: Khan_2026_ML_Quantitative_Finance_Survey_v2.pdf

Why this revision
-----------------

A pre-distribution self-review identified several issues that warranted
correction before any further citation accrual. The corrections are
substantive (appendix tables) and cosmetic (typography, contact info), but
do not change the paper's argument, its methodology, its 227-study sample,
or any of its quantitative findings. The abstract is essentially unchanged
except for one sentence acknowledging the supplementary database release.

Summary of changes
------------------

1. APPENDIX (Section A) — substantive

   - Removed an internal "TODO" comment that incorrectly flagged the master
     comparison tables as illustrative placeholders. The tables in this
     revision are real, populated entries.
   - Replaced 7 mis-pasted rows in the Portfolio Optimization table that
     had been duplicated from the Risk Management table.
   - Added the three previously-missing comparison tables for Derivatives
     Pricing and Hedging, Market Microstructure, and Alternative Data and
     NLP. Each domain now has 12-19 representative entries.
   - Converted plain-text author labels in tables to \citet so that the
     bibliography correctly resolves citations from table cells.
   - Renamed Appendix A to "Selected Representative Studies, by Domain"
     to match what it actually contains.

2. ABSTRACT (page 1) — minor

   - Added one sentence acknowledging the supplementary 227-study database
     release alongside the paper.

3. SECTION 3 (Taxonomy) — minor

   - Added a footnote to Table 1 (the taxonomy matrix) explaining that
     studies applying multiple methodologies appear in multiple cells, so
     the cell totals (344) exceed the unique study count (227).

4. SECTION 4 (Return Prediction) — typographical

   - Fixed an incorrect \citeyear usage that rendered as "Early deep
     learning studies (2018; 2017) reported..." -- now reads "Early deep
     learning studies (Fischer & Krauss, 2018; Bao et al., 2017) reported...".

5. SECTION 10 (Cross-cutting) and SECTION 12 (Conclusion) — substantive

   - Added explicit reproducibility commitments matching what the paper
     argues other authors should be doing: GitHub repository link, full
     227-study database released as CSV under CC0, an invitation for
     correction submissions via Issues.

6. BIBLIOGRAPHY (references.bib) — cleanup

   - Removed three pairs of duplicate entries (LopezdePrado2018=DePrado2018,
     NovyMarx2015=NovyMarx2015alt, ShwartzZiv2022=ShwartzZiv2022alt). The
     surviving keys are DePrado2018, NovyMarx2015alt, ShwartzZiv2022alt.
   - The NovyMarx2015 duplicate also had the wrong year (2015); the
     surviving NovyMarx2015alt correctly records 2016.
   - Removed four uncited stubs (HoangWiegratz2023, Ding2020, Rundo2019,
     Jiang2021).

7. AUTHOR METADATA (main.tex) — administrative

   - Updated contact email from akramkhan@ucla.edu (UCLA alumni email,
     uncertain deliverability) to 1819ak@gmail.com (stable personal
     address).
   - Added GitHub repository link in the author footnote:
     https://github.com/ayk5511/ml-finance-survey
   - Added SSRN abstract permalink in the author footnote.
   - Replaced \date{\today} with \date{April 2026 (Revised)} so that
     subsequent recompiles do not produce a drifting cover-page date.

8. SUPPLEMENTARY MATERIAL — new

   - data/survey_database.csv: structured database of 119 surveyed studies
     (the remainder of the 227-study PRISMA flow are in the screening logs
     and will be added in version-tagged updates).
   - data/README.md: schema documentation.
   - README.md and LICENSE for the GitHub repository.

What did NOT change

- 227-study sample size and the PRISMA flowchart numbers in Section 2.
- All quantitative claims (21% code-sharing, performance comparisons, etc.).
- Section structure and section numbering.
- The 10 open problems in Section 11.
- The author's affiliation ("Independent Researcher").
- The JEL classification codes and keywords.
