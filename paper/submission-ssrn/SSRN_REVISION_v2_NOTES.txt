SSRN Revision Notes for Paper 1, Version 2
============================================

Title: Machine Learning in Quantitative Finance: A Systematic Review of
       Methods, Applications, and Open Challenges (2015-2025)

SSRN Abstract ID: 6562398
Author: Akram Khan
Original submission: 2026-04-12
Revision date: 2026-04-27
File: Khan_2026_ML_Quantitative_Finance_Survey_v2.pdf

Why this revision
-----------------

A pre-distribution self-review identified several issues that warranted
correction before any further citation accrual. The corrections include
both substantive additions (a new methodological contribution: the
Reproducibility Disclosure Score, applied to 99 empirical studies) and
style cleanup (typography, contact info, prose tightening). The 227-study
sample and the qualitative findings are unchanged; the abstract now adds
two sentences flagging the new RDS contribution and the rewritten
Section 11.10 on audit trails for regulator-defensible AI.

Summary of changes
------------------

1. APPENDIX (Section A) — substantive

   - Removed an internal "TODO" comment that incorrectly flagged the master
     comparison tables as illustrative placeholders. The tables in this
     revision are real, populated entries.
   - Replaced 7 mis-pasted rows in the Portfolio Optimization table that
     had been duplicated from the Risk Management table.
   - Added the three previously-missing comparison tables for Derivatives
     Pricing and Hedging, Market Microstructure, and Alternative Data and
     NLP. Each domain now has 12-19 representative entries.
   - Converted plain-text author labels in tables to \citet so that the

   - Renamed Appendix A to "Selected Representative Studies, by Domain"

2. ABSTRACT (page 1) — minor

   - Added one sentence acknowledging the supplementary 227-study database

3. SECTION 3 (Taxonomy) — minor

   - Fixed an incorrect \citeyear usage that rendered as "Early deep

What did NOT change
-------------------

- 227-study sample size and the PRISMA flowchart numbers in Section 2.
- All quantitative claims (21% code-sharing, performance comparisons, etc.).
- Section structure and section numbering.
- The 10 open problems in Section 11.
- The author's affiliation ("Independent Researcher").
- The JEL classification codes and keywords.

9. NEW SUBSECTION 10.5 — substantive

   - Added "The Reproducibility Disclosure Score" subsection with a
     defined 0-2 rubric (+1 for code public, +1 for openly accessible
     or fully simulated data) applied to 99 of the 119 empirical
     studies in the released database. New Table 5 reports the
     distribution by application domain, venue type, and era. Headline:
     mean RDS 0.78 on the 0-2 scale; only 16% disclose both code and
     public/simulated data. The per-study scores ship in the
     supplementary CSV (data/survey_database.csv) under a new column
     rds_score. This turns the survey from a description into a
     reusable measurement instrument.

10. SECTION 11.10 — replaced

    - The original "Quantum Computing (Eventually)" subsection was
      filler, admitted as not-actionable in its own opening sentence.
      Replaced with "Audit Trails for Regulator-Defensible AI", which
      surfaces a concrete open research problem at the intersection of
      SR 11-7 / OCC 2011-12 / EU AI Act compliance requirements and the
      lack of audit-grade artifacts produced by current ML training
      pipelines. The replacement is a closer fit to the paper's
      reproducibility theme and is more useful to practitioners.

11. STYLE CLEANUP — minor

    - Replaced 24 in-prose em-dash parenthetical pairs with commas,
      parentheses, semicolons, or restructured sentences. Bullet-list
      em-dash separators (formatted as "\item \textbf{X} --- Y") were
      preserved as standard typography.
    - Removed AI-fingerprint phrases: "we believe" (1), "comprehensive"
      (1), "paradigm shift" (1), "ad hoc" (1), "robust" (1), where the
      original did not need them.
